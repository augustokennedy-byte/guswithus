Why GUS exists

Every kid deserves someone in their corner. We make sure they have one.

Our mission is to empower underserved youth and communities through transformative programs that promote personal growth, strengthen unity, and cultivate long-term success — through education, support services, and skill-building opportunities.

Our story began with the Police Athletic League — the program that shaped our founder's own path. GUS exists to pay that forward.

Four programs, one goal

From basic needs to big futures

Each program removes a different barrier standing between a young person and their potential.

🧥
Dignity first

Clothing & Basic Essentials

Relief and dignity through access to everyday necessities — because no child should sit in class worrying about what they don't have.

  • Free clothing for youth and families in need
  • Seasonal drives: school uniforms, coats, shoes
  • Hygiene kits and essential household items
📚
Confidence in the classroom

Academic Support & Mentorship

Helping K–12 students build skills, self-esteem, and the habit of advocating for themselves — in school and beyond.

  • Tutoring and homework help for K–12
  • Mentorship in leadership and goal setting
  • Workshops: study skills, financial literacy, future planning
🏀
Character through sport

Athletic Development & Wellness

Basketball, golf, and floor hockey as vehicles for discipline, teamwork, and mental and physical health.

  • Organized youth sports programming
  • Clinics and fitness events that build wellness
  • Safe, structured spaces for recreation and mentorship
🛠️
Skills that pay forward

Vocational Training & Careers

Equipping young adults with practical skills and connections for sustainable success after graduation.

  • Job readiness: resumes, interviews, workplace skills
  • Career exploration and trade training
  • Pathways to internships, certifications, and jobs

Meet the founder

Built by someone who lived it

Gus is a New York City educator, coach, and youth mentor. A former NYC Department of Education employee, he coaches basketball, golf, and floor hockey, and works daily alongside guidance counselors helping teenagers become self-advocates.

His own trajectory was shaped by the Police Athletic League — proof that one program, one mentor, one season can change a life. GUS was founded to be that program for the next generation, with current NYC DOE educators serving on its staff and board.
